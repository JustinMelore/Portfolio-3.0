Our Light

Though the world may be darkened and corrupted by loneliness, a simple act of kindness can bring light to a gloomy world. As you face the challenges and puzzles of life, remember that kindness can be returned. Our Light is a puzzle game that plays into the theme of sharing your light.

Play Game
Platform: Windows
Engine: Unity
Development Duration: January - May 2025
Project Role: Lead Engineer

In Our Light, the player travels through a gloomy landscape using the special light from their lantern to overcome obstacles, such as creating bridges of light to cross gaps or clearing out dense fog. However, your light is limited, and if you do not use it carefully, you will succumb to the darkness around you. Luckily, you do not have to go alone, as there are others you can meet who will help share your burden if you share your light with them.

Post-Mortem

As the sole engineer for this project, I was responsible for designing all of the mechanics and systems that went into the game. By extension, I was also responsible for all of the implementation/coding of those systems and mechanics. Additionally, since most of the remaining members of our team had very little programming experience, it was also my job to ensure that our systems were as easily modifiable and intuitive as possible so as to allow our designers to make any necessary tweaks.

Many of the project's strengths came from its relatively simple concept combined with the scalability of its mechanics. As a very simple puzzle game, it didn't necessitate the creation of many complex systems outside of the individual puzzle mechanics, which made development fairly straightforward. Additionally, use of inheritance allowed for our game's mechanics to be very easily extended for future additions. This meant that much of the technical work was completed fairly early in the development timeline, which allowed for some extra time dedicated to quality assurance and concepting for future mechanics.

With that being said, our limited development time and the team's inexperience with the Unity game engine meant that the project did run into problems involving scope. Much of the content that we wanted to include in the final game, including additional puzzle mechanics and NPC companions, had to be substantially reduced compared to our original vision. UI also ended up being an area of struggle, with various screen size-related bugs popping up throughout development.

In the future, I will definitely use my experience on this project to get a better sense of scope when planning out features and mechanics so as to not leave certain parts of the game feeling unfinished. Additionally, I will make sure to place a greater emphasis on separation of concerns when designing scripts, as there were certain parts of the game's code that needed to be moved to separate scripts later into development as the logic became too complex for its original location. More effective separation at the beginning of development would have helped reduce this problem and is something I will keep in mind going forward.
